HCI Project

The Good Place

A substantial number of students at Wellesley College are struggling with mental health challenges; however, accessing the required resources often proves to be difficult. "The Good Place" is an app crafted to address this issue and bolster student mental health. Students can utilize "The Good Place" as an emotion tracker, a digital support animal, and a tool to navigate through mental health resources

Wellesley Ask Nico

Wellesley Ask Nico is an interactive FAQ platform designed for Wellesley College students, inspired by the Wellesley FAQ Facebook Group. It allows users to ask and answer academic questions, with responses from peers and an AI trained on official Wellesley resources and personalized user profiles. Features include a robust tagging system for easy navigation and community moderation through upvotes and vouches. More than just a help site, it's a collaborative space to share knowledge across the Wellesley community.

BOB - Tangible User Interfaces

College students often face high levels of stress from long hours and looming deadlines, which can lead to serious mental health issues like anxiety and depression. At the same time, there's a growing trend of students collecting comforting toys, such as Jellycats, for stress relief. Our research explores using these toys as tangible user interfaces (TUIs) to support student well-being. Unlike digital self-care apps that can feel intrusive, TUIs offer a physical, engaging, and non-disruptive way to promote self-care, using the comforting presence of toys to encourage stress-reducing habits.
